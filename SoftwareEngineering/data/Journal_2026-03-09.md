Chris Pirillo used AI to write a tool that
[scans our handwriting](https://mastodon.social/@ChrisPirillo/116190380761042358)
and creates a font from it.  You fill out a grid with letters and symbols, and
his app running in your browser generates the font files.  My first attempt to
draw the characters using a fountain pen didn't turn out great.  Many letters
were missing.  I tried a second time, using a fine point Sharpie instead and
adjusting the scan to boost contrast, and this time it worked.  I might be able
to create a lighter weight font with the fountain pen coupled with boosted
contrast.  I modified this blog to use the font for a handwritten feel.

Chris claims he produced the app over a weekend with vibe coding.  Opinions are
divided in the comments on Mastodon over the use of AI.  I'm just pointing out
he used AI to create the app.  The app does not appear to use AI to create the
fonts.  It claims everything is done in the browser.
